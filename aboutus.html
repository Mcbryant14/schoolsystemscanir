Greg Stockton, President

Gregory R. Stockton is President and Founder of Stockton Infrared Thermographic Services, Inc.™ Based in Randleman, NC. Greg has been a practicing infrared thermographer since 1989. He is a Certified Infrared Thermographer with twenty-six years experience in the construction industry, specializing in maintenance and energy-related technologies. Mr. Stockton has published eleven technical papers on the subject of infrared thermography and written numerous articles about applications for infrared thermography in trade publications. He is a member of the Program Committee of SPIE (Society of Photo-Optical Instrumentation Engineers) Thermosense and Chairman of the Buildings & Infrastructures Session at the Defense and Security Symposium.

Alejandro Tache

Alejandro Tache is the Aerial Thermographer Interpreter for AITscan™, Stockton Infrared Thermographic Services, Inc.’s Aerial Infrared Division. Mr. Tache has been with the company since 2005 and has extensive experience as a combat pilot, test pilot and flight instructor. He has a degree in Aeronautics Science from Columbian Air Force Academy. His superior performance as a pilot, thermographer, and software analyst led to his appointment as Aerial Thermographer Interpreter.

Eric Stockton, ElectriSCAN™ Director

Eric Stockton is Division Director of Stockton’s ElectriSCAN™ and MechaniSCAN™ divisions. Eric has been with the company since 1996 and has been an integral part of its success and growth. Eric R. Stockton received a BA in Zoology from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill in 1982. He was an environmental consultant for Carolina Power and Light’s Shearon Harris Nuclear Power Plant for 14 years prior to joining SITS.

Jefre Cockerham

Jefre Cockerham is Stockton Infrared’s Special Projects Director. Jef supports all of Stockton’s divisions by managing special projects. His extensive infrared experience includes twenty-five years in advanced technology industries in the role of infrared researcher and program manager positions at Pratt & Whitney, Lockheed Martin, Lucent Technologies and Rolls-Royce. Special projects worked include design, assembly and operation of a fully automated measurement system for an infrared signature simulator (MWIR imager, LWIR imager, and spectral radiometer), and diversified engineering support for the F-15 SMTD/F-16/YF-22/YF-23/F-22/F-35/Delta Clipper/DD-21 Blue Team/V-22 programs.

Ron Predmesky, IRresearcher™ Director

Ron Predmesky is the Division Director for IRresearcher™ and ProcessSCAN™. Mr. Predmesky joined SITS in July, 2005. Prior to that, Ron spent 32 years as a research engineer for Ford Motor Company. His duties included work in tire testing, infrared weld inspections (spot, stir friction and ultrasonic), process control, Ford GT heat management, brake inspection, composite structures inspection, adhesives inspection and building facilities operations. During his tenure with Ford, he served as chairman of Ford Infrared Users Group and was an instructor of Level I and Level II infrared certification classes.
